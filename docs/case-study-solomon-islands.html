The Case of the Solomon Islands: Chinese Penetration and the Reshaping of the Pacific

Executive Summary

The Solomon Islands have emerged as a critical node in Indo‑Pacific strategic competition. China has consolidated influence through economic investment, political alignment, and a security agreement that reshapes the regional balance. The case illustrates an incremental “anchoring strategy” that challenges Australia’s and the United States’ traditional role in the South Pacific.
